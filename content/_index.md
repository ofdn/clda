This is a workshop series on recording and archiving Adivasi, Tribal and mother tongues and local languages with lesser resources (*there is more resource for official languages like Hindi, Tamil, Bangla, Kannada, Gujarati and Telugu*). Apply **before July 15** if you already record and archive such languages, or want to start. Less experience is okay.

The series has:

- seven online workshops (May–August 2026)
- one in‑person workshop in Kochi, Kerala (3 September 2026)

**[Apply here](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfZwQqQgSGA8LvN_-ZW-GbI-N2jhq40BwEL4BbuL2Im2egIig/viewform?usp=header)**

## Steps

1. Check [eligibility](#eligibility).
2. Apply from link above.
   - There will be 2 types of sessions: 7 online sessions from May to August, and an in-person workshop in Kochi, Kerala on 3 September 2026. Select the one you can attend.
   - Each online session will last 1 hour. It will happen on Mondays (18 May, 1 June, 15 June, 29 June, 13 July, 27 July and 10 August) at 6 in evening.

## Eligibility

- You record or plan to record Adivasi/Tribal/other mother tongues with lesser resources (preference will be given to native speakers).
- You are a Wikimedian interested in audio‑video documentation of such languages.

## If You Get Selected

- If attending online, you will attend at least 1 full session.
- If attending in Kochi, you will attend at least 3 full online sessions and in person from 9 AM to 6 PM.
- Online/in-person sessions will be interactive, not lectures. You are required to read and participate actively.
- You will contribute to Wikimedia projects and share some work in a safe and ethical way.

## What You Will Learn

In audio‑video recording and archiving:

1. **18 May**: How to plan a community language documentation project.
2. **1 Jun**: How to handle consent, credit, and payment fairly.
3. **15 Jun**: How to record clear audio and video in real‑world conditions.
4. **29 June**: How to manage and back up files safely.
5. **13 July**: How to edit media for sharing and long‑term use.
6. **27 July**: How to prepare media and metadata for Wikimedia Commons, Wikidata, Wikipedia, and other Wikimedia projects.
7. **10 August**: How to share materials with archives, libraries, and communities.

## Outputs

The series will produce open resources, and we will share them with you:

- a curriculum for community language documentation and archiving, in simple English and ready for translation
- recorded talks and tool demonstrations (online and in‑person sessions)
- documentation for OpenSpeaks tools, including a subtitling tool
- updated guidance for a framework to help plan your documentation and archiving
- a tested text style guide for captioning, subtitling and transcribing
- example workflows from recording to Wikimedia upload and external archives

You will be credited as authors for the materials you create or improve.

## Outcome

- When we finish this series, participants will very well know how to document and archive important things from their community in audio and video.
- They will also know how to contribute some outputs to Wikimedia projects.
- They will have practical knowledge of tools and educational guides for these.
- This workshop model will be clearly written here. Anyone can organise a similar workshop for their own community using this model.
